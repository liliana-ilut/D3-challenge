Correlations Between Smokers vs. Age

The data set is based on 2014 ACS 1-year estimates( https://factfinder.census.gov/faces/nav/jsf/pages/searchresults.xhtml ) and includes data on rates of income, obesity, poverty, etc. by state.

The above visualization is a correlation between Smokers vs Age per State in United States.

One obervable trend is that people tend to smoke more when they are in their middle 30's to early 40's. There are a few outliers: UT seems to have a lower median age (30.5) of smokers and ME has the highest median age (44.1). UT has the lowest percentage of smokers (9.7%) and WV has the highest percentage (26.7%).
